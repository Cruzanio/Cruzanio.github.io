From Fuzes to Functions

My Military Service

I became a member of the Air Force in April 2015. My Air Force Specialty Code was 2W0X1, a Munitions Systems Specialist. From maintaining small arms to missiles and bombs, the job taught me invaluable principles in regards to attention to detail as well as personal accountability. With 4 grateful years in Aviano, Italy filled with eye-widening cultural learning and deployments, I returned to the United States to continue my service at Hill AFB. During my 5th year of service I came to realize the time to further my personal goals had arrived. I began the transition to the Air National Guard with a college plan in place.

The Career Transition

In March 2020, I was accepted into the University of Texas at San Antonio to pursue a degree in Psychology. COVID restrictions followed shortly thereafter which changed my career pathway entirely. I began to look into alternative education options while also reconsidering my field of study. In the end I decided to enroll in a software bootcamp called Codeup starting that July in an attempt to enter the world of technology. Codeup was an intensive, full-time, 22-week full stack Java accelator program that equipped me with the skills necessary to be a proficient web developer.

New Beginnings

After graduation in February 2021, I landed my first role as a Junior Application Developer with Accenture. Over the course of my year and a half tenure I gained experience across a multitude of roles. As an Application Developer, I utilized various programming languages including Python, TypeScript, and Java. I also worked with several frameworks, such as Radzen, Angular, Bootstrap, and Spring Boot. I later took on the role of Junior DevOps Engineer. In this position, I became proficient in source code management using tools like Git and Gerrit. I also gained experience in product deployment orchestration with Docker and Jenkins. Additionally, I worked with infrastructure as code using software such as Terraform and Ansible. The experience gained from these rolls pathed the way for my next role as a Software Contractor.

Leveraging and Learning

In July of 2022 I took on a new role as a Software Contractor with a focus in defense work. Over the course of my year long contract I was able to hone my skills as a software developer while also leveraging my military experience throughout my day to day activities. This time of my career was expecially momentous as I procurred a niche skillset in Geospatial Information Systems (GIS) which opened a multitude of doors therafter. I advanced my skills across various technologies, including Java and Python, while also becoming proficient in React.js and gaining expertise in concepts like multithreading and low-level communication software.

Today...

I work as a Senior Software Engineer at L3Harris with a focus in frontend development and backend aircraft telecommuncation services. My primary tech stack in everyday use includes React, Java, and MySQL. Each day is filled with fresh challenges and innovative processes to develop cutting edge mission systems for both civilian and military aircraft fleets. The collaborative work environment, combined with the demanding client expectations and tight deadlines, allows me to leverage my military experience in team management and apply efficient programming practices to ultimately accomplish the mission.
